The Playground

Welcome to the Playground! This section is much less visually-friendly than the Tour, so if this interface is indimidating or confusing, start with the Tour and come back! This page is doing a lot of complex audio processing so it is saving all its energy for what you hear instead of what you see.

This is a place to explore, experiment, and most of all... play!

A Quick Warning

Please feel free to browse the selection of pre-loaded audio samples in the 'SAMPLE' buttons. There are 15 unique sounds loaded into this page. If you do want to hear a sound, please wait a second or two after pushing the 'SAMPLE' button and BEFORE pressing 'PLAY SAMPLE' button on the very right of the screen. It takes a second to load the new sound into the player, and pushing the button too early might confuse the program. If you do want to load a stock sample, and it's not playing just refresh the page, or try a new one and come back.

Recording Input

If you want to hear your own voice, or any sounds you have available to you, just hit the 'RECORD' button on the right side of the screen. While the circle is red, record any sounds you want, then hit the button again to stop recording. The button will then change and say 'PLAY RECORDING'. It will then play and loop whatever has been recorded and stored in your browser's temporary memory.

No part of Playing In The Waves is saving any recordings made on this site. Feel free to check my work for yourself. All audio is saved only on YOUR device, then deleted as soon as you refresh the page, or turn off the browser.

Still don't believe me or your device not letting you record? Use the stock samples!

Recording Output

If you want to record the output of the page to download and save, click that button in the top-right part of the screen just for that purpose. Any sounds that happen while the circle is red will be captured, then when you're ready push the button again to stop recording and start the file download.

The file will download as a .webm file. I understand this is not a usual file type and may not be immediately handy to use, but there are plenty of free to use websites that will handle this conversion for you. Here's one that I have tried and works great.

What To Do

You'll notice there are three tracks available, each with its own set of stock samples and effects. Feel free to record something new into each one, or explore the sounds provided. Activating an effect will bring up a blue circle above to let you know it is working. Each effect, except for REVERB and REVERSE, is being slightly modulated by an LFO, or has an LFO being modulated by another LFO. There is motion in the effects by default so you can sample lots of kinds of sounds easily and without having to know exactly what you want from the start.

...Wait, Can I Click On Those Blue Circles?

Yes!

If you DO want more control, click on those blue circles above the active effects. They will then turn pink, then yellow, then back to blue as you keep clicking on them. This will turn off the default LFO and give you control over the effect itself. See how now a pink or yellow line is connected to your mouse on the screen? This means your mouse is directly controlling a specific component of that effect. You can connect multiple effects to your mouse and wave them around as you like.

Pink means the effect is connected to the mouse's x-axis position on screen, and yellow is the mouse's y-axis position. Try having a pink and yellow line at the same time from multiple effects and hear how moving the mouse diagonally will effect each of them differently.

Once you find a specific place for an effect that sounds good to your ears, press the SPACE BAR. This will freeze in place any effects that are currently attached to the mouse at that time. Then your mouse is free to stick another effect parameter somewhere else! Click on the colored circle to cycle through default, x-axis, and y-axis control. If an effect is frozen, reset it by turning the effect off and on, or clicking the circle above it to cycle to another mode.

Key:

  • BLUE = Default Effect (no user control over effect modulation)
  • PINK = X-Axis Control
  • YELLOW = Y-Axis Control
  • SPACEBAR = Freeze pink or yellow effect to that part of the screen to lock effect in place
